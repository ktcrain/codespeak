The 6 Rules of Algospeak

1
Acronym Reduction

Sensitive multi-word phrases collapse into initials. These are the most widely understood and stable substitutions: SA (sexual assault) · DV (domestic violence) · CSA (child sexual abuse) · SH (self-harm) · ED (eating disorder) · TW (trigger warning) · MH (mental health).

2
"Unalive" Substitution

All language around death, killing, and suicide converges on unalive. Conjugates naturally: unalived, unaliving, unalive themselves. This is the single most universally adopted algospeak word.

3
Rhyme / Sound-Alike Substitution

Replace flagged words with phonetically similar, innocuous words. The community chose these together: rape → grape · sex → seggs · sexual → seggsu4l · porn → corn · weed → le lettuce · cocaine → le snow. Conjugations follow the same swap (raped → grape'd).

4
Leet-Speak Vowel / Letter Swap

Swap one or two letters with visually similar numbers or symbols so the word is human-readable but defeats keyword matching: a → 4 · e → 3 · i → 1 · o → 0. Used when there's no good rhyme: r4cism, 4buse, depr3ssion, tr4uma.

5
Mid-Word Break (Dash / Symbol / Space)

Insert a dash, dot, asterisk, or space inside a word to break the string without changing pronunciation: gr∞ming, h4te-crime, p3d0, g*n. Works as a last resort when rules 1–4 don't apply.

6
"Le / Le petit" Prefix (French Camouflage)

Prepending the French article le or le petit signals irony / coded language to in-group viewers while appearing benign to classifiers: le herb (marijuana) · le lettuce (weed) · le petit harm (child abuse). Used sparingly for nouns only.
